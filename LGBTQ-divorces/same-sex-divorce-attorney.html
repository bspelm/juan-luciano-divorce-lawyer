In general, same-sex couples who get divorced face the same issues as every other married couple—dividing marital property, child custody and visitation, spousal support and/or maintenance, and child support. In some states, there are specific guidelines that differentiate between same-sex and heterosexual divorce cases, but as a general rule, the process is the same for all. If one or both spouses are residing in the state of New York, they can file for a divorce after the filing of several forms with the Supreme Court of the State of New York. These forms include Summons with Notice (Form UD-1) and the Verified Complaint. The summons is served to the spouse to notify them of the divorce proceedings and the complaint is filed with the details of the allegations. It's critical to have a same sex divorce attorney navigate through these forms to avoid any legal pitfalls.

One of the most complicated aspects of divorce for same-sex couples involves child custody and visitation, which are usually determined by what is best for the children. A same sex divorce attorney will be adept at navigating these complex situations, ensuring that both parties' parental rights are recognized and upheld.

Aside from the complexities of child custody and visitation, the other main issue that arises with same-sex divorce is property division. When a married couple has substantial assets that they have accrued over the course of their marriage, it can be very difficult to determine what is considered marital and what is separate. This is especially true when the marriage has lasted longer than traditional heterosexual marriages. Having a same sex divorce attorney can help ensure a fair and equitable division of these assets.

Legal Representation for Same-Sex Divorce in New York

The end of a marriage can be one of the most challenging experiences that couples go through, especially when children are involved. Even when the decision to divorce is mutual, it can still be a very difficult situation for all parties. However, for LGBTQ+ couples who are considering divorce, the process can be even more complicated due to state laws and traditions that do not always fully recognize same-sex marriages and relationships. This can have an impact on various aspects of divorce proceedings, including asset and debt division, spousal maintenance (commonly known as “alimony”), and custody decisions, among other things. For this reason, consulting a same sex divorce attorney early in the process is crucial for navigating the unique challenges faced by LGBTQ+ couples.

In general, New York treats same-sex marriages the same as traditional marriages, with the only requirement being that at least one spouse has lived in the state for a minimum of one year. This means that, if a same-sex couple decides to divorce, they will have to file a petition for separation and define their terms of separation just like any other heterosexual married couple. They can also choose to pursue a contested or uncontested divorce, depending on their needs and goals for the future. Having a knowledgeable same sex divorce attorney by their side can make a significant difference in achieving a fair and equitable resolution.

However, if they have children, then they will need to determine child custody and visitation arrangements as well. The involvement of a same sex divorce attorney is particularly beneficial in such cases, as they can help navigate the complexities surrounding parental rights and custody agreements that are equitable and in the best interest of the children.

Furthermore, if they have accumulated marital assets during their relationship, then they will need to decide how these assets will be split up according to New York’s equitable distribution protocol. This can be particularly complicated for same-sex couples, since many of these relationships have lasted years prior to their official legal recognition as marriages, meaning that there could be a significant amount of co-mingled property to sort through. A same sex divorce attorney with experience in asset division can ensure that the distribution is handled fairly and considers the unique circumstances of the couple.

Finally, if a same-sex divorce has any child support components, this can be a tricky area as well. While a court will generally only award a fair and reasonable amount of child support, it is important for all parties to be prepared before entering into discussions about this subject. The guidance of a same sex divorce attorney can be crucial for ensuring that the agreement is as beneficial to both parties as possible, ensuring that all legal and financial aspects of child support are thoroughly addressed.
