Understanding the Role of Separation Lawyers in New York

There are a number of different ways to end a marriage, including divorce and legal separation. While both options have their own perks, there are certain benefits to pursuing a separation agreement instead of a divorce. One of the most important is that it typically saves time and money. While divorce cases tend to move at a snail's pace and court appearances can be "hurry up and wait" affairs, a separation agreement can often allow you and your spouse to control the timetable for negotiations. Consulting with a legal separation attorney can further streamline this process, ensuring all legal aspects are properly handled.

In addition, a separation agreement may allow you to resolve matters such as custody and visitation arrangements for any children involved, as well as financial issues like property division and spousal support before your case becomes entangled in a year-long waiting period that is required by law for a divorce. Moreover, since a separation agreement is considered a binding contract, it is generally possible to incorporate this contract into a final divorce judgment, making the process of dissolving your marriage much smoother. A legal separation attorney can assist in drafting this document to meet both parties' needs effectively.

A legal separation attorney can help you and your spouse work through the details of your situation by drafting a separation agreement, which is essentially a detailed contract between both parties regarding all aspects of their marriage relationship. These include living arrangements, responsibilities, rights, and entitlements involving any children involved and a variety of other matters that are addressed through mediation sessions. Once the agreement is drafted, it is signed by both parties and notarized before being filed with the Family Court in the county where the married couple lives.

The agreement will also typically contain terms that specify that both parties have been legally separated for a minimum of one year. This is significant because under New York law, a year of legal separation is a prerequisite to filing for a divorce. It is therefore imperative that couples consider the consequences of a legal separation before taking this route, particularly because it can lead to a divorce after only a year of marriage. A legal separation attorney is crucial in clarifying these terms and ensuring that they are legally enforceable.

Some people choose legal separation instead of a divorce for religious reasons or to try to work things out in their marriage. However, this is not a way to avoid the need to file for divorce in the future. Divorce can only be granted on specific grounds and requires specific wait periods, while legal separation is simply a temporary measure that can eventually lead to a divorce if the marriage does not recover. A legal separation attorney can explain these distinctions and help you understand the potential implications for your specific situation.

Understanding New York Legal Separation

When married couples in New York decide to separate, they can choose from two options: divorce or legal separation. A legal separation can resolve all of the issues related to a divorce, including property division, child custody, and support. In addition, spouses who obtain a legal separation can later convert it into a full divorce. This allows them to retain certain benefits, such as health insurance and military or social security benefits that would otherwise terminate if they divorce. Consulting a legal separation attorney can provide clarity on how these benefits might be impacted.

In order to get a legal separation, couples must first draft a written agreement that establishes all of the details of their separation. This includes a statement of all assets that both parties own, how they will divide those assets, and how each party will pay their share of ongoing expenses. The couple must also agree on child custody and support arrangements, ensuring that the best interests of the children are considered. A legal separation attorney can assist in drafting this agreement to ensure it meets legal standards and addresses all necessary areas comprehensively.

The agreement must be voluntarily entered into by both parties and must be approved by the judge. The legal separation will remain in effect until the couple either reconciles or divorces. However, if the couple wishes to change the terms of their legal separation, they can do so by filing an amended agreement with the court. A legal separation attorney can facilitate these changes, ensuring they are filed correctly and reflect the couple’s current wishes.

The key difference between a legal separation and a divorce is that a divorce entails ending the marital partnership by dissolving the marriage and distributing assets equitably. This is not possible with a legal separation, which only provides spouses with a written agreement that resolves all of the same issues that a divorce does.
